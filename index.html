The problem

Most people cannot see a rip until it has them.

A rip current is a narrow channel of water flowing away from shore. It does not splash or roar. It often looks like the calmest gap between the waves, and that is exactly what makes it dangerous.

How it works

Trained eyes on the water, held in your hand.

Step 01

It uses cameras that already exist.

RipCo reads existing public beach camera feeds. No new hardware, no drones. The cameras are already pointed at the water.

Step 02

Computer vision reads the surf.

The model analyses the feed frame by frame and looks for the visual signature of a rip: a darker channel, a gap in the breaking waves, foam pulling out to sea.

Step 03

You get a warning you can read at a glance.

When a likely rip is found, RipCo outlines it in red with a confidence percentage, and points you back to the flags and the lifeguards.
