No. RipCo is an extra pair of eyes, not a verdict on the water. Lifeguards and the red and yellow flags are the standard you swim by. The app exists to back them up and warn you earlier, never to replace them.

Every detection carries a confidence percentage, and that is a reading, not a promise. RipCo can miss rips and can flag features that are not rips. Nothing in the app is ever presented as an all-clear, so treat every session as though a rip could be present.

Detection needs a usable camera image. At night, in heavy rain or in strong glare, detection quality drops or becomes unavailable, and the app says so plainly rather than guessing. Conditions data remains available, and the rules do not change: flags and lifeguards first.

RipCo starts at Manly Beach and is built to scale to other Australian beaches. Coverage depends on a beach having a public camera feed the app can read.
