How it works

From a public camera to a warning in your hand.

RipCo does not need new cameras or new hardware. It reads feeds that already watch the water, identifies rip currents with computer vision, and shows them as a clear visual warning. Here is the whole pipeline.

The detection pipeline

Step 01

It uses existing public cameras.

RipCo connects to public beach camera feeds that are already watching the water. There is no new hardware to install, no drones and nothing for you to set up. If a beach has a public camera, RipCo can work with it.

Step 02

AI computer vision identifies rips.

The model analyses the feed frame by frame, looking for the visual signature of a rip: a darker channel cutting out through the surf, a gap in the breaking waves, and foam or sediment streaming away from shore.

Step 03

You get a clear visual warning.

When a likely rip is found, it is outlined in red on the live view with a confidence percentage, so you can see where it is and how sure the model is, then act on it before you get in the water.

What it is, and what it is not

An aid, not a guarantee.

A confidence percentage is a reading, not a promise. No model is perfect, and RipCo is built to be honest about that.

It can miss a rip

Detection depends on what the camera can see. Light, angle, weather and distance all affect it, so a rip can be present without being detected.

Conditions change quickly

Rips form and shift in minutes. A calmer-looking stretch of water can become a rip, and the smoothest gap between the waves is often where one is running.

The flags come first

Whatever RipCo shows, swim between the red and yellow flags and follow the lifeguards. They are reading the water in person, and they always have the final word.
